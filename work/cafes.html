Sip City Stories

A web app with a collection of the best coffee shops in Brooklyn for studying and work-from-home with an aim to spotlight culturally vibrant spaces.

Background

SipCityStories was born from the need to provide students with curated study spaces in downtown Brooklyn, recognizing the importance of a conducive environment in an area often visited for its tourist attractions like the Brooklyn Bridge and other iconic landmarks.

Role

User research, human-centered design, visual design.

Time

5 weeks, Feb. - April, 2024

User Research

To uncover culturally vibrant coffee shops for Sip City Stories, I meticulously analyzed Google Reviews and Yelp. This helped me identify cafes celebrated for their cultural offerings, authenticity, popularity, and student-friendly amenities like reliable Wi-Fi.

Additionally, I visited local coffee shops, engaging directly with diverse communities and exploring their rich cultural history. This hands-on research helped me curate a selection of cafes that celebrate diversity while providing ideal study environments for students.

Data Obervations

I started by categorizing my observations from shop visits into two distinct groups: pros and cons. These categories were specifically tailored to highlight the cultural influence and suitability for students within each cafe.

Visual Design

Figma wireframe and sketches.

Colors & Typography

Sip City Stories' branding strikes a balance between functionality and warmth, aiming to evoke a cozy ambiance. The color palette, featuring Alabaster, Silver Chalice, Brown Bramble, Shades of Gray and Rose Taupe, embodies this sentiment. Additionally, I chose to use Trebuchet MS font as it reflects simplicity and readability, enhancing the website's functionality.

What I Learned

✔ User Engagement and Research: The project emphasized direct engagement with users and thorough research to understand their preferences beyond assumptions. Visits and discussions with café owners complemented interviews, ensuring the final product resonated authentically with a broader audience.

✔ Research Application and Learning: This project marked a pivotal transition from research to practical implementation. It reinforced the crucial link between theory and practice, teaching how to translate abstract ideas into actionable solutions grounded in real-world contexts.

✔ Inspiration from Diverse Sources: Drawing inspiration from various sources, including Brooklyn's coffee shop architecture, enriched the visual identity of Sip City Stories. Exploring the cultural significance of these spaces guided design decisions, creating an authentic and engaging user experience.
