Fengshui (风水)

Feng shui (say: fung sh-way),meaning 'wind and water' in Mandarin Chinese, is an ancient art of arranging objects to achieve harmony and balance in a space.

Background

This Feng Shui web app was developed to bring the ancient art of Feng Shui into the digital age, offering users common tips to harmonize their living spaces. Inspired by the challenge of optimizing small living spaces in NYC, it provides practical advice to create a balanced and positive bedroom environment.

User Research

Before settling on our topic, we thoroughly explored diverse domains, with each of us individually brainstorming five areas of expertise. For each domain, we listed 5 specific topics we could teach interactively in under 10 minutes, considering media and quiz questions. From these 25 potential topics, we selected our top 6 favorites.

Group Topics

  • Basic feng shui principles to declutter tiny apartments
  • How to properly greet others in different cultures
  • The correct technique for exercises on a squat rack
  • Borrowed words from other languages (aka loanwords)
  • Basic rules of football/soccer
  • Animal-Related English Idioms

After considering various topics, we collectively decided to proceed with Fengshui. We conducted ethnographic interview with a classmate and then presented our concept to a designated user, Amelia.

Amelia

@ Barnard College

Introducing our user Amelia, a young professional on the brink of her final year of college and recently planted in the vibrant landscape of New York City. Amelia is navigating the transition from dormitory life to the independence of her own apartment with excitement and determination. With a desire to cultivate a serene and balanced living environment, Amelia seeks practical solutions to optimize her new urban dwelling.

Quotes

From our interviews, we gathered valuable insights that provided us with a glimpse into how our users (including Amelia) perceive the utility of this concept and the challenges they currently face, which this solution could potentially address. Here are some noteworthy observations.

❝ I move frequently due to my studies and am particularly organized about certain objects like mirrors and wall decor, but I really need guidance on furniture placement in new spaces. ❞

❝ Living in a small NYC apartment, I often struggle to find ways to make my space feel more open and inviting. ❞

❝ I've heard about Feng Shui but never knew how to apply it practically, especially in a limited space. ❞

❝ As a Chinese American, Feng Shui has always been a part of my cultural heritage. I grew up hearing about its principles from my family, but I never fully understood how to apply them in my own living spaces. ❞

Subsequently, drawing from feedback provided by our users and insights gleaned from competitor analysis, we pinpointed the challenges and advantages associated with implementing the web app.

Design the information hierarchy, color scheme and fonts.

Color Design 1-2 Screen Types

The color design for the homepage and first Feng Shui concept utilizes a soft base color complemented by accent colors, aiming to evoke tranquility and sophistication while ensuring visual consistency.

Color Design 3-4 Screen Types

The color design for the self-check pages and final score page uses bold and accent colors to enhance clarity, maintain consistency, and provide distinct visual cues for user assessment and completion.

Information Design #1

Design the information hierarchy for the homepage and first Feng Shui concept, emphasizing the logical flow of information to enhance user understanding.

Information Design #2

Design the information hierarchy for the self-check pages and final score pages, ensuring conceptual groups are clearly outlined for intuitive navigation and easy comprehension.

Colors + Font

We opted for the Spectral font for its classical and sophisticated appearance, reflecting the ancient origins of feng shui. Our goal is to emulate the style of traditional scriptures, while Spectral's efficiency enables text-rich and long-form reading.
